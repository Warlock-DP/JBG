THE ORIGIN STORY

The Engineer

It started with code, not campaigns.

An engineer who spent years building systems — writing logic, debugging at 3 AM, obsessing over efficiency. The kind of person who automated his own morning routine before most people hit snooze. But something was missing. The systems worked. The businesses using them didn't grow.

The Revelation

Marketing wasn't magic. It was a broken machine.

The deeper he went into the marketing world, the more he saw the cracks. Agencies overcharging. Businesses guessing. Small owners drowning in tools they didn't understand. Everyone was selling "digital transformation" — nobody was actually transforming anything.

The Fusion

What if you brought an engineer's brain to marketing — and let AI do the heavy lifting?

That was the moment. Not a boardroom epiphany. A 2 AM realisation fuelled by chai and frustration. Blend the jugaad mindset — that raw, resourceful, "figure-it-out" energy — with the precision of AI. Don't just market. Build growth engines.

Jarwix Is Born

Elevate with AI. Not a shortcut that cuts corners — a smarter path that removes the unnecessary ones.

Jarwix became the answer to every business owner who said "I know I need to be online, I just don't know how." An AI-powered, no-BS growth partner. Part engineer. Part marketer. All hustle.

"We didn't start Jarwix to be another agency. We started it because every agency we saw was doing it wrong."

Illustration Style

Illustrations are used sparingly — only when photography or icons can't communicate the concept. Every illustration must feel like part of the Jarwix system.

VOICE & TONE

We sound confident, not arrogant. Clear, not clever. Every word earns its place.

✓ DO
  • → Short, punchy sentences. Two beats, then impact.
  • → Use contrast and parallel structure.
  • → Frame AI as what competitors already use.
  • → Sound confident and decisive.
  • → Lead with outcomes, not features.
✗ DON'T
  • → No corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, ecosystem).
  • → No hype or ALL-CAPS shouting.
  • → No over-explaining — cut anything with three commas.
  • → Never start with "In today's fast-paced world..."
  • → Don't use passive voice when active works.

Sound Like This

"Automation isn't the future. Your competitors are already using it."

"We build the systems. You build the business."

"You don't need more tools. You need the right system."

MOTION & ANIMATION

Every movement should feel intentional. Fast in, smooth out. Never bouncy, never sluggish.

Ease Out

cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)

Primary easing. Use for entrances, reveals, and page transitions. Starts fast, decelerates naturally.

Spring

cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)

Micro-interactions only. Buttons, toggles, small elements. Slight overshoot adds life.

Linear

linear

Progress bars and continuous animations only. Never for UI elements.

Motion Principles

01

Duration

200–400ms for micro-interactions. 400–800ms for section reveals. Never exceed 1s.

02

Direction

Elements enter from bottom (translateY +20px). Never from the sides. Fade + translate, always together.

03

Stagger

Grid items stagger at 100ms intervals. Max 5 items staggered per group. Beyond that, animate as one.

04

Reduced Motion

Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Disable all transforms, keep opacity fades at 0ms duration.
